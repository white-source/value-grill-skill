# Evidence Ledger and Board Conclusion Templates

## Evidence Classes

| Class | Definition |
|---|---|
| `Fact` | A source-backed claim including publication date and source location |
| `Management statement` | A company claim not independently verified |
| `Inference` | An operating conclusion derived from cited facts |
| `Assumption` | A model input that could be falsified |
| `Unknown` | Material information without reliable support |

Every time-sensitive fact must include an as-of date. Never present an inference or assumption as a fact.

## Cash-Flow Driver Tree

```
Revenue = price × volume × mix
Gross profit = revenue − variable cost
Operating profit = gross profit − fixed cost
Operating cash flow = operating profit + non-cash charges − working-capital investment
  where working-capital investment = Δ(receivables + inventory − payables)
Free cash flow = operating cash flow − maintenance CapEx − growth CapEx
ROIC = (operating profit × (1 − tax rate)) / (debt + equity − cash)
```

## Board Conclusion

1. **Customer-payment-to-FCF mechanism**: How does the business turn customer payments into free cash flow?
2. **Stable and changing conditions over 5, 10, and 20 years**: What operating assumptions are likely to hold versus shift?
3. **Bear, base, and bull conditions**: Scenario drivers, transmission paths, and falsification criteria. Never a single pseudo-precise forecast.
4. **Up to three FCF drivers**: Key operating levers and their transmission mechanisms.
5. **Counterevidence and unknowns**: The most dangerous evidence that would trigger reassessment, and unknowns ranked by materiality.
6. **Next board agenda**: Required role, evidence to obtain, and operating metric to observe.
