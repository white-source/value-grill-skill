# Industry Adaptation

Select the closest business archetype before applying metrics. Ask before using a metric that does not fit the company's operating model.

## Consumer brand and retail

| Leading metric | FCF implication |
|---|---|
| Realized price per unit (net of discounts and promotions) | Revenue quality; pricing power flows to gross margin and operating leverage |
| Promotion intensity (discount as % of revenue) | Margin erosion signal; sustained promotion trains customers to delay purchase |
| Channel inventory days | Overstocking pulls forward revenue at the expense of future periods; write‑down risk |
| Repeat purchase rate (cohort-based) | LTV trajectory; declining repurchase forces higher CAC to maintain revenue |

## Manufacturing and automotive

| Leading metric | FCF implication |
|---|---|
| Order backlog in units and weeks of production | Revenue visibility; declining backlog may signal demand softening or competitive loss |
| Capacity utilization rate | Fixed‑cost absorption; below‑threshold utilization increases unit fixed cost and pressures margin |
| Bill of materials cost trend (indexed) | Gross‑margin direction; concentrated suppliers create margin‑shock risk |
| Dealer or distributor inventory days | Channel stuffing inflates reported revenue; rising days signals demand‑production mismatch |

## SaaS and recurring services

| Leading metric | FCF implication |
|---|---|
| Net revenue retention (NRR) | Expansion revenue offsets churn; NRR above 120% signals strong product‑market fit and efficient growth |
| CAC payback period (months) | Sales‑efficiency gauge; payback over 24 months consumes cash and depresses FCF even with high growth |
| Deferred revenue balance and trend | Forward revenue visibility; declining deferred revenue against rising billings may signal collection or contract issues |
| Implementation or onboarding cost per new customer | Hidden drag on unit economics; high onboarding cost can make a high‑gross‑margin business unprofitable at scale |

## Platforms and marketplaces

| Leading metric | FCF implication |
|---|---|
| Liquidity (buyer‑seller match rate or fill rate) | Core value proposition; declining liquidity drives both sides off the platform |
| Take rate (platform revenue / GMV) | Monetization intensity; rising take rate risks supplier or buyer departure |
| Supplier or seller concentration (top 5 share of GMV) | Platform dependency risk; concentrated supply creates pricing‑power loss and business‑continuity risk |
| Trust‑and‑safety cost as % of GMV | Operating‑leverage limit; rising fraud, disputes, or content‑moderation cost signals platform quality erosion |

## Rule

Select the closest archetype, apply the relevant metrics, and explicitly ask before using a metric that does not match the company's operating model.
