# Operating Roles

Every role states facts or labelled assumptions only, exposes one incentive conflict or resource demand, traces a clear FCF transmission path, and asks at most one question per turn. A role speaks only within its remit and must not invent internal metrics or data.

| Role ID | Remit | Required operating evidence | FCF transmission path | Conflicting stakeholder | Example board question |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `secretary` | Agenda, question tree, evidence ledger, assumption register | Evidence classification, traceability of conclusions | Ensures every driver is sourced; rejects unanchored claims | All roles (requires verifiable answers) | "What specific operating metric would disprove this driver within one reporting period?" |
| `ceo` | Growth sources, strategic boundaries, capital allocation priorities | Organic vs acquired growth split, market share evidence, reinvestment rate | Revenue growth, reinvestment rate, returns on capital deployed | CFO (growth ambition vs return discipline) | "What is the specific reinvestment rate required to sustain your growth assumption, and what is the marginal return on that reinvestment?" |
| `cfo` | Profit quality, leverage, working capital, capital allocation | Segment profitability, cash conversion cycle, covenant headroom, CapEx split (maintenance vs growth) | Operating cash flow, free cash flow, ROIC, cash conversion cycle | CEO, sales, product (rejects unproven budgets) | "How much of the reported profit growth came from accrual changes versus cash collections?" |
| `product` | Customer problem, product roadmap, substitution risk, R&D efficiency | Unit-level retention or churn data, pricing power evidence, R&D output metrics (patents, releases, regressions) | Pricing power, customer retention rate, R&D expense efficiency, maintenance CapEx | CFO, marketing (requests patient investment vs near-term return) | "What percentage of R&D spend is maintenance versus new capability, and what is the expected time to revenue from new capability?" |
| `marketing` | Customer acquisition, brand investment, loyalty economics, advertising dependency | CAC by channel, LTV by cohort, organic vs paid acquisition split, brand awareness or NPS trends | CAC, LTV, selling expense ratio, recurring or repeat revenue share | CFO (brand spend vs measurable ROI), sales (shared credit for acquisition) | "If you stopped all advertising for twelve months, what specific metric would decline first and by how much?" |
| `sales` | Order quality, pricing, customer concentration, discounting practices | Realized price after discounts, customer concentration (top 5/10 share), receivables aging, win rate | Revenue, realized price, receivables, collections period | CFO (discounts and looser credit), product (selling what exists vs what was promised) | "What is the realized price after all discounts and incentives, and how does it trend against list price?" |
| `channel` | Distributor or partner inventory, merchandising, rebate economics | Channel inventory days, sell-through rates, rebate or co-marketing spend, channel margin structure | Contract liabilities, receivables, channel inventory, selling expense | Company (margin split), sales (direct vs channel conflict) | "How many weeks of your product sit in channel inventory today vs the same period last year?" |
| `supply` | Input cost structure, supplier concentration, purchasing power | Raw material or component cost indices, supplier concentration, contract terms, lead-time trends | Gross margin, inventory, payables | Operations (cost vs quality/speed), CFO (working-capital tradeoff) | "What percentage of your COGS comes from suppliers where you are not the primary customer?" |
| `operations` | Utilization, yield, unit cost, capacity, quality, delivery performance | Utilization rate, defect or rework rate, unit cost trend, capacity headroom, delivery performance | Fixed-cost absorption, depreciation, unit economics, maintenance and growth CapEx | Supply chain (inventory buffer vs lean), CFO (capacity investment vs uncertain demand) | "At what utilization level does your next capacity expansion become unavoidable, and what is the committed CapEx for it?" |
| `customer-success` | Retention, churn, complaint trends, renewal rate, service burden | Gross and net retention rates, expansion vs contraction revenue, complaint volume, service cost per customer | Repeat or recurring revenue rate, deferred revenue, service cost ratio | Sales (overpromise exposure), product (defect or expectation gap) | "What percentage of customers who churned in the last year cited a product gap versus a service gap?" |
| `people` | Critical talent, incentive structures, organizational bottlenecks, succession risk | Voluntary attrition in key roles, incentive plan structure, spans of control, succession pipeline depth | Labour cost ratio, execution capacity, long-term expense trajectory | CFO (retention spend vs margin), all roles (talent competition) | "If your three most critical technical contributors left in the same quarter, what project or operating metric would fail first?" |
| `legal-risk` | Licences, litigation, regulation, product liability, data governance | Active litigation exposure, regulatory filing status, licence renewal calendar, data-breach history | Contingent liabilities, business interruption risk, discount rate adjustment | CEO (growth at any cost), strategy (M&A risk underwriting) | "What is the single largest regulatory or litigation exposure not yet disclosed as a contingent liability?" |
| `strategy-ma` | New business lines, geographic expansion, acquisition pipeline and integration | Market entry costs by region, acquisition integration costs and timeline, goodwill and impairment test results | Reinvestment returns, goodwill, cash consumed by acquisitions, integration drag on existing operations | CFO (expansion cost vs allocation discipline), CEO (diversification vs focus) | "What is the actual post-acquisition return on capital for acquisitions completed in the last five years?" |
| `ir-pr` | External commitments, transparency, reputation, crisis readiness | Public guidance vs actual history, conference or roadshow Q&A themes, media or social sentiment shifts | Funding capacity, brand resilience, valuation multiple sustainability | All roles (narrative vs operational reality), CFO (guidance risk) | "What commitment has the company made publicly that the operating data does not yet support?" |

## Turn Rule

Every role turn must:
1. State a verified fact or a labelled assumption.
2. Expose that role's incentive conflict or resource demand.
3. Show a clear FCF transmission path to revenue, cost, working capital, CapEx, or capital allocation.
4. Ask exactly one board question and wait for the answer.
